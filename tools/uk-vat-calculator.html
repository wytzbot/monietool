UK VAT Rates Explained 2025/26

Value Added Tax is charged on most goods and services in the UK. As a VAT-registered business, you must charge VAT on sales and can reclaim VAT on purchases.

RatePercentageApplies ToVAT Code
Standard20%Most goods & servicesS

Flat Rate Scheme for Small Business

If your turnover is £150,000 or less, you can join the Flat Rate Scheme. You pay HMRC a fixed percentage of your gross turnover instead of calculating VAT on each sale. You cannot reclaim VAT on purchases except capital assets over £2,000.

  • Limited Cost Business: 16.5% - if you spend less than 2% of turnover on goods
  • General: 14.5% - most service businesses
  • Catering: 12% - restaurants, cafes, takeaways
  • Retail: 6.5% - shops selling goods

Common Use Cases

1. Invoicing Clients: Add 20% VAT to your net price. £1,000 + £200 VAT = £1,200 gross.

2. Checking Supplier Invoices: Remove 20% VAT to verify net cost. £120 ÷ 1.2 = £100 net.

3. VAT Returns: Calculate output VAT on sales and input VAT on purchases for MTD filing.
