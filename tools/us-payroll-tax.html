W-2 vs 1099: Tax Comparison

W-2 Employee: Employer pays 7.65% FICA. You pay 7.65%. Eligible for unemployment, workers comp, health benefits. Employer withholds taxes.

1099 Contractor: You pay full 15.3% self-employment tax. Can deduct business expenses: home office, mileage, equipment. Must pay quarterly estimated taxes. No benefits.

Rule of Thumb: 1099 rate should be 25-30% higher than W-2 to cover self-employment tax and benefits. $60k W-2 ≈ $78k 1099 for same take-home.

State Income Tax by State

Nine states have no income tax: Alaska, Florida, Nevada, New Hampshire, South Dakota, Tennessee, Texas, Washington, Wyoming. California has highest top rate at 13.3%. Most states use flat or progressive brackets 2-10%.

Frequently Asked Questions

The 2026 wage base is $176,100. You pay 6.2% Social Security tax on wages up to this amount. Wages above $176,100 are exempt from Social Security tax but still subject to 1.45% Medicare tax.
Generally, you pay tax to the state where you physically work. If you live in Texas but work remotely for a California company, you pay no state tax. Some states have reciprocity agreements. Check your state’s DOR website.
FUTA is Federal Unemployment Tax: 6% on first $7,000 of wages, usually reduced to 0.6% after state credits. SUTA is State Unemployment Tax, paid by employers. Rates vary by state and employer experience rating.
Q1: April 15, Q2: June 15, Q3: September 15, Q4: January 15. Pay via IRS Direct Pay or EFTPS. Penalty applies if you owe $1,000+ at filing and didn’t pay estimates.
