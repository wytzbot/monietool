HST vs GST+PST: What's the Difference?

HST - Harmonized Sales Tax: Single combined tax. Administered by CRA. One rate, one remittance. Used in ON, NB, NL, NS, PE.

GST+PST: Separate federal and provincial taxes. GST 5% goes to CRA. PST goes to province. Rates vary. Used in BC, SK, MB, QC.

Quebec QST Rule: QST 9.975% is calculated on the GST-inclusive amount. Example: $100 × 1.05 = $105. $105 × 9.975% = $10.47 QST. Total = $115.47.

Zero-Rated & Exempt Items

Zero-Rated 0%: Basic groceries, prescription drugs, medical devices, feminine hygiene products. You charge 0% but can claim input tax credits.

Exempt: Residential rent, most health/medical services, educational services, financial services. No tax charged, no input tax credits.
